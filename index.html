Project Idea

Overview

My project idea is an app called $ale$ in which the user is given the ability to find the sales that their favourite brands would be offering online or in store. Through which I aim to achieve an overall app that allows me to give users the convenience and efficiency of finding their favourite products regardless of the type of product. By doing so, this app will be a step up from the Honey browser extension as it allows users to search up an item they wish to buy after which the app shows them the item of their wish on sale, and the location of the sale online or in store, all the while Honey only offer coupons for online shopping Initial problems I anticipate to face are a good amount of downloads for my app at the start and a steady incline from there, furthermore the advanced skills that will be required by me to use the coding language Java and tools like MIT app inventor.

Motivation

This app would be in high demand as its an app that allows users to get their favourite products for a cheaper price then its normal price. This will do well in the app store as its a relatable thing for everyone. The app will consist of every product sold in shops, excluding houses, cars, manual labour etc. for one to see sales on. This is projected to do well as sales are something that attracts everyone due to the amount of money one saves. Furthermore, its more convenient for users as it allows them to have all the sales in one place, thus removing the effort of going to individual websites to find sales in stores. As imagined, every customer prefers to purchase items mostly while they are on sale, making it likely to be a point of success for my app. Furthermore, after trying to scour the app store and internet, I only came across Honey which was somewhat relevant to what I plan on making. However Honey, only allows coupons for online purchases and those purchases that it sees the user attempting to purchase, although there is a search function in honey for coupons, it is all online. My app will attempt to play on impulse buying and coerce the user to buying products that they were looking at but didn’t have intentions of buying in store and online.

Description

I aim to do this through the layout and features of the app, the initial steps the user will have to take to use the app will include allowing ‘$ale$’ to see their location while using the app. After which they would enter their personal information like Name, Email, Phone Number. After this the user will be shown the mainframe of the app in which it will have 5 option to the app on the bottom layout. First option includes a vertical list of products that have been put on sale by shops all around their state, in condescending order of most recent to latest sales. This will feed the impulse buying likelihood, as this page will show all sales near the user spanned out across all types of products. The second option will be a search option in which the user can enter the specific product they aim to get on a sale. After which the app will dish out the item on sale, the location and the sale price. This allows the user to directly benefit from the app as they will be able to find the item of their choose in a affordable price. Third option is a self select in which there will be a list of what sort of item they want (technology, clothing, shoes, jewellery). After which they can pick the price they want to pay and the system will show a list of that category of item and in condescending order of the desired price from closest to furthest. The fourth option will be a location wheel in which, the user will have shopping malls highlighted around them and upon clicking one, the app will show all the sales happening in the shops of the shopping mall. Once the user has selected a shop, it will show them the items on sale and their sale price. This will save a lot of aimless scouring in a shopping mall by customers. The last and fifth option in the app will be a Wishlist in which the users are able to input their desired products. The Wishlist can be as broad as a category of an item or as specific as the exact brand of it. Users can add the product to their wish-list if the product they wished for wasn’t on sale, or the sale wasn’t big enough for them to want to buy it. When added to wish-list, $ale$ will send a notification for when the product has a sale on it or when the desired discount is on.
